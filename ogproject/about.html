About AMTS

India's largest Municipal Transport Service serving Ahmedabad since 1947

Our Organization

Committed to connecting Ahmedabad through sustainable transport

Amdavad Municipal Transport Service

Regd. Office: Transport House, Jamalpur, Ahmedabad – 380022

AMTS is India's largest Municipal Transport Service for local conveyance. It is a voluntary service managed by Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation under Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act. AMTS runs the public bus service in the city of Ahmedabad.

The responsibility of the administration of AMTS comes under the Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation. AMTS is managed by Transport Manager under transport committee and Municipal Corporation from the very inception, the organization has adopted service oriented attitude.

AMTS Historical Timeline

Key milestones in our journey since 1940

1940

First Resolution Passed

The first resolution was passed on 10th June 1940 in the General Board meeting to begin transport services (Resolution No. 476). A special committee was formed to map out bus routes and acquire necessary loans.

1947

Service Launch

On 1st April 1947, AMTS launched with 60 buses. It was the first public sector city bus service in India. Initial fare was 1 Anna (6 paise) minimum and 3 Annas (20 paise) maximum.

1950

Financial Separation

AMTS accounts were separated from municipal funds on 1st August 1950, with separate budgeting beginning from 1951-52.

1955

Politeness Week

First "Politeness Week" celebrated from 21-27 March 1955, inaugurated by the Mayor at Lal Darwaja, recognizing exemplary staff.

1960

Expansion to Suburbs

Routes extended to Kathwada, Lambha, Ranip, Hathijan, Vanch, Ramol, Nikol and Amli Road, connecting villages to the city.

Present

Current Operations

Today AMTS operates one of Gujarat's largest bus networks with:

  • 1,250+ buses in fleet
  • 450+ bus routes
  • 5.5 lakh daily passengers
  • 15,000+ km daily coverage
